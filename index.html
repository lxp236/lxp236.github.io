Established in 2001 on the basis of the optical specialty of the former Department of Physics, the Institute of Modern Optics (IMU) of Peking University is an important supporting force for the State Key Laboratory of Artificial Microstructure and Mesoscopic Physics and the Frontier Science Center of Nano-Optics of the Ministry of Education, and a main supporting unit for the Yangtze River Delta Optoelectronic Science Research Institute of Peking University. With the mission of improving innovation capacity and serving the major national demands, and the goal of building world-class disciplines, the Institute sets up two disciplines: optics and atomic and molecular physics, and forms competitive research directions in mesoscopic optics and micro-nano photonics, ultrafast optics and molecular and atomic strong field physics, quantum optics and nonlinear optics, and optoelectronic functional materials and device physics. The Institute's influence at home and abroad is increasing, and it has made important contributions to the "Double First-Class" construction of Peking University. The Institute always adheres to the construction of high-quality scientific research team, and has an innovation group in optics of the Department of Mathematics and Science of the National Foundation Committee and an innovation team in key fields of the Ministry of Science and Technology. Through the cultivation and introduction of a group of outstanding young scholars, the research team has developed rapidly in the past two decades, and the scientific research level has gradually improved. At present, there are 30 permanent staffs in IOP, including 1 academician of Chinese Academy of Sciences, 4 special professors of Changjiang River, 11 outstanding youth fund recipients, 3 leading talents of Ten Thousand Talents Plan, 12 outstanding youth talents of national talents plan. The "Extreme Optics Research Innovation Team" was awarded the title of "The Sixth National Advanced Collective of Professional and Technical Talents". Members of IOP have made remarkable achievements in their respective fields and have been recognized by their peers at home and abroad. In the past five years, more than 100 academic papers have been published in Nature, Science and its sub-journals and other international top publications. Many members of IOP have been elected as fellows of Chinese Optics Society (COS), American Optics Society (OSA), International Society of Optical Engineering (SPIE) and British Physical Society (IOP), and have served as chief editors, editorial board members and chairmen of important academic conferences of important journals at home and abroad.
